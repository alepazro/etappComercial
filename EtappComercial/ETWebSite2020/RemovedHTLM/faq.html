INSTALLATION: How is it installed? (Top)

Installation is pretty similar to installing a stereo to your vehicle. The GPS Tracker can be hidden, usually inside the dashboard of the vehicle. It has to be hard-wired to the electrical system of the vehicle. The typical installation takes 3 cables: Power cable, Ground cable, and a third cable that is connected to a line that has power while the Ignition is ON and that has no power while the Ignition is OFF.


Another part of the installation is the placement of the GPS antenna. This antenna can be concealed right underneath the dashboard, but never below metal, as the GPS signal will not be received through metal. It can be placed below plastic, fiber glass, glass.


For most customers we recommend getting the GPS Tracker installed. We recommend installations at a Best Buy store, a Car stereo/alarms shop, or almost any mechanic should be able to do it. There is also a website called https://www.vehicleinstallers.com/ where you can find professional GPS installers close to your area.


Installation usually cost between $50 and $75 per vehicle and may take up to 30 minutes per vehicle.

Can you track in real time? (Top)

Yes, our system provides Real Time Tracking. Now, in the GPS Industry, Real Time does not mean Instant tracking. It means a location update every certain of minutes. In our case, we offer minute by minute tracking, which means every single minute you will receive an update of the location of your vehicles, while they are moving. When the vehicles have the Ignition OFF, our trackers go to a slower interval to save the vehicle's battery, and only report every 60 minutes.

Yes, easiTrack offer Breadcrumb Trail, which shows the trail where the vehicle has been in a given timeframe.
What is a Geofence? (Top)

A Geofence is a virtual boundary that you can create around a given location. With easiTrack you can create as many geofences as you need. Most typical geofences are created around places of Customers, Vendors, Job Sites, Yards, etc. What is interesting about Geofences is that the system will trigger and send you alerts every time a vehicle enters or leaves a geofence, providing an electronic, automatic log of places your vehicles have visited.

About installation

Can I install the device myself? (Top)

Yes, you can. You will need some tools and ideally some experience in this type of installation, as it involves hardwiring the tracker to your vehicle's electric system.
What are the two antennas for? (Top)

The GPS Tracker comes with two antennas: One antenna will be to receive the GPS signals from the satellites to allow the tracker to perform location calculations. The other antenna, called the GSM antenna or the GPRS antenna, is used to transmit the information from the device to our data center. Both antennas play a critical role in the proper operation of the GPS Tracker.
What is the best location for the antennas? (Top)

The GPS antenna has to be installed in a location with direct view of the sky. For a concealed installation, the GPS antenna can be hidden in a place where it will not have any metal above it. It can be hidden right underneath th dashboard, as usually its material is fiberglass or plastic. The GSM antenna can be installed in any location.
How long does it take to install? (Top)

A typical installation may last around 20 to 30 minutes. If you want to install Inputs (to trigger events when a door is open/close, for example), then estimate aproximatelly 20 additional minutes per each input.
How much does the installation cost? (Top)

Installation performed by a professional may cost between $50 up to $75 for a basic installation. If you want to install some inputs, each input will add about $25.
Where is the GPS Tracker installed? (Top)

Most of our cutomers conceal the GPS Trackers right underneath the dashboard. Many professional installers will suggest other locations depending on your vehicle's particular configuration.
